// 01 · About us

APMLAB

APMLAB — the Structural and Computational Biophysics of Membrane Proteins and Membrane Active Peptides Lab — is a research group at the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona (UAB), Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology.

We combine coarse-grained and atomistic molecular dynamics simulations, in silico drug discovery, and experimental biophysics to decode the molecular determinants of membrane protein function and peptide–membrane interactions, with applications ranging from ion channel pharmacology to neurodegenerative disease.

All our code, datasets, and simulation protocols are openly available — we are committed to reproducible, FAIR science.

// 02 · Research

Research projects

2.1 · MEMBRANE PROTEINS

Membrane Proteins

We study the structure, dynamics, and function of integral membrane proteins — with a focus on TRP ion channels (TRPV2) and P4-ATPases. Using coarse-grained MD simulations and cryo-EM, we characterise lipid fingerprints, drug binding sites, and conformational transitions relevant to cardiovascular and pain physiology.

2.2 · MEMBRANE ACTIVE PEPTIDES

Membrane Active Peptides

We investigate how neuropeptides, cell-penetrating peptides, and amyloidogenic peptides — including Dynorphin A, Aβ, and angiotensin II — interact with and disrupt lipid bilayers. Our work informs the molecular understanding of neurodegeneration and peptide-based therapeutics.
